We are a group who identify as females and have a passion for technology.

We are committed to expanding the number of women in tech in the Springfield area, facilitating connections and collaborations within the community, and providing learning opportunities across various technical subjects.

Meet the ladies behind the screen.

Jordan Griffin

Jordan is a Quality Advocate at Asynchrony Labs. She has a passion for coding, the Springfield community, and her family. Her career began by building friendships in the tech world as she bartended in downtown Springfield, wondering where to go after getting a degree in Animation at MSU and unsure of pursuing that career path. She started SGF WIT to fill a gap in the area for women to come together and support each other in the tech world, as well as supply guidance for those who don't know all the possibilities. Jordan is excited to help break stereotypes about technology and spread the love of tech to more women.

Hannah Gavisk

Hannah is a Software Engineer at Gravitate Solutions where she enjoys working with a variety of technologies, including .NET and Python. She loves to learn new things, whether by reading, trying something new, or connecting with and learning from others. Hannah is thrilled to be a part of Springfield Women in Technology for that very reason - to bring together women with a love of learning and technology and to make connections that will help us all grow.

She also co-founded The Geek Foundation, a nonprofit organization that seeks to increase the tech employee pipeline by making it more diverse. Savannah Russell

Savannah is a Front-End Developer at 40Digits, where she was the first woman to work at the company. She advocates for accessibility while continuing to dance on the leading edge of front-end development principles. She joined Springfield Women in Tech to help build a community and connect with and share experiences with those who may also be one of a lone few in their workplace. When not endlessly refreshing Twitter, she can be found helping officiate roller derby bouts, in rehearsal for the musical groups she's a part of, planning Greater Ozarks Pridefest, or spending time outdoors.
